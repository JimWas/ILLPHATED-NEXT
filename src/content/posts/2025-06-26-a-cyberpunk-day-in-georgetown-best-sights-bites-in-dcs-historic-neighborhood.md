Morning Stroll & History

🌿 C&O Canal towpath – Start your day walking the scenic C&O Canal, a peaceful greenway where you can rent a kayak or simply stroll past colorful canal-side townhouses and historic stone locks .
Historic homes & cobblestones – Wander along O and M Streets, lined with 18th‑century homes, cobblestones and old streetcar tracks—Georgetown feels like stepping into a living history book .


---

Midday Eats & Waterfront Views

Yellow Café or Baked & Wired – Recharge with a perfectly brewed coffee, Levantine‑inspired pastry, or famous cupcake from this beloved local bakery .
Filomena Ristorante – For a hearty lunch, indulge in fresh‑made pasta at this Italian‑American mainstay by the canal. The warm, “grandma’s kitchen” ambiance has attracted presidents and dignitaries for decades .
Georgetown Waterfront Park / Tony & Joe’s / Nick’s Riverside – Afterward, head down to the waterfront. Whether you grab a seat by the fountain at the park or sip a glass of rosé at Tony & Joe’s, the river views and breezes are unbeatable .


---

Afternoon Cultural Stops

Dumbarton Oaks – Wander through 27 acres of manicured gardens—complete with ponds, fountains, and secret pathways—a hidden oasis that feels like “The Secret Garden” .
Tudor Place – Just up the hill, this stately estate offers insight into Georgetown’s historic elite, beautiful gardens, and early‑American architecture .
Martin’s Tavern – Pop in for a quick seat at the booth where JFK famously proposed to Jackie in 1953. Even if you’re not ordering a meal, the photo-ops are worth it .


---

Dinner & Evening Entertainment

Fiola Mare or Sequoia – Treat yourself to seafood with a view. These standout waterfront restaurants pair fresh catches with luxe atmosphere and scenic Potomac panoramas .
Blues Alley – Cap off the day with live jazz in this legendary alleyway club. Since 1965, it’s hosted everyone from Ella Fitzgerald to Dizzy Gillespie .

Tips to Maximize Your Day

Arrive early to snag free street parking or use nearby Metro + Uber/Lyft.

Book ahead for canalside boat tours and dinner reservations, especially on weekends.

Mix outdoor + indoor: start with fresh air, wrap up in cozy restaurants and jazz bars as the sun sets.
