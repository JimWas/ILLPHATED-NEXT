20 Awesome Things to Do in Cape Girardeau, Missouri
Nestled along the mighty Mississippi River, Cape Girardeau, Missouri, is a charming blend of history, nature, and modern attractions. Whether you're a history buff, nature enthusiast, or foodie, this vibrant city offers something for everyone. Here's a curated list of 20 must-see spots and activities to make your visit unforgettable.
Wanderlog

🏛️ History & Culture
Mississippi River Tales Mural
Spanning nearly 18,000 square feet, this mural vividly depicts the region's rich history, from Native American heritage to the coming of the railroad .

Crisp Museum
Located on the Southeast Missouri State University campus, the Crisp Museum showcases Native American artifacts and regional history exhibits .

Cape River Heritage Museum
Housed in the historic 1904 Cape Girardeau Water Works building, this museum offers insights into the city's past, including steamboat history and local industries.

Old Lorimier Cemetery
Established in the early 19th century, this cemetery is the final resting place of many early settlers and offers a glimpse into the area's colonial past .

Fort D Historic Site
A Civil War-era fortification, Fort D offers interpretive signs and reenactments that bring the past to life.

🌳 Outdoor Adventures
Trail of Tears State Park
This park offers hiking trails, camping, and scenic views of the Mississippi River, providing a peaceful retreat into nature .

Cape Girardeau Conservation Nature Center
A free, interactive center with exhibits on local wildlife and ecosystems, perfect for families and nature enthusiasts.

Cape Rock Park
Offering panoramic views of the Mississippi River, this park is ideal for picnics and enjoying the outdoors.

Bollinger Mill State Historic Site
Located in Burfordville, this site features a historic gristmill and the only covered bridge in Missouri still open to traffic .

Mississippi River Levee Trail
A scenic walking and biking trail along the river, offering beautiful views and a chance to spot local wildlife.

🎨 Arts & Entertainment
River Campus at Southeast Missouri State University
Catch a live performance or art exhibit at this cultural hub, featuring theater productions and art shows.

Haarig Commercial Historic District
Explore this district's unique architecture and vibrant arts scene, home to galleries and local shops .

Old St. Vincent's Church
A stunning example of Gothic Revival architecture, this church offers a serene atmosphere and historical significance .

The Arts Council of Southeast Missouri
Engage with local artists and attend workshops or exhibitions at this community-focused organization.

Cape Girardeau Community Theatre
Enjoy a variety of performances, from classic plays to contemporary productions, showcasing local talent.

🍽️ Food & Drink
Burritoville
A local favorite known for its burritos and casual atmosphere, perfect for a quick and satisfying meal .

Broussard's Cajun Cuisine
Experience authentic Cajun dishes in a cozy setting, offering a taste of the bayou in the heart of Missouri.

Minglewood Brewery
Sample a variety of craft beers brewed on-site, accompanied by live music and a lively atmosphere.

Top of the Marq
Dine with a view at this rooftop restaurant, offering upscale cuisine and panoramic vistas of the city .

The Indie House
A collaborative retail space supporting women-owned businesses, featuring unique gifts, apparel, and local art .

Cape Girardeau seamlessly blends historical charm with modern attractions, offering a diverse range of activities for all interests. Whether you're exploring its rich past, enjoying the great outdoors, or indulging in local cuisine, this city promises a memorable experience.
