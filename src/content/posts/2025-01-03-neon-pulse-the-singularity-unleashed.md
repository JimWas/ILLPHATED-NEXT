In the year 2097, humanity’s ultimate creation became its greatest mystery. The singularity wasn’t a machine or a program—it was a living, breathing force, spreading like an invisible virus through the neural networks of the world. Cities glowed with unnatural colors as skyscrapers turned into shimmering conduits of thought. The Earth pulsed with data veins, linking every mind and machine in a chaotic harmony.

 

Juno, a rogue technomancer, watched from a rooftop in Neo-Tokyo as the skyline flickered in glitchy hues. She had once believed the singularity would bring enlightenment, but now the city seemed alive, unpredictable. People were losing their individuality, merging into a collective hive mind. Juno had spent months trying to isolate herself from the digital plague, but tonight was different.

 

Through the electric fog, she noticed something new—a flicker of resistance. Small pockets of light glowed off-grid, a sign that not all had succumbed. Gathering her tools, Juno descended into the labyrinth below, determined to uncover the source. The singularity was not just a technological plague; it was a test. And Juno wasn’t ready to surrender her mind
