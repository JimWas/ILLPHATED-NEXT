The rain hissed as it hit the neon-lit pavement of Old Sector 19. A thousand holograms flickered in the sky, blending advertisements and lost dreams. Beneath the glow of a towering magenta sign that read “永恒联盟” — Eternal Union — a lone figure approached.

He wore a wide-brimmed hat, the kind outlaws once wore on dusty plains, now reinforced with carbon-thread mesh. His duster coat was stained with ion burns and the grit of offworld dust. They called him Rex Aether, the last of the free-range space cowboys.

But Rex hadn’t come for a duel or a bounty. Not this time.

Across the street, the ground trembled under the weight of something ancient and massive — an elephant. But this wasn’t some zoo relic. This was Unit EL-PH4NT, a cybernetic envoy from the Martian Elephant Syndicate. Towering and plated in chrome with glowing violet eyes, it was both beast and machine, diplomat and warrior.

Their worlds had collided at the edge of a star war. A skirmish over asteroid rights in the Kepler Expanse nearly brought extinction to both species. Now, amid the drizzle of data rain and the hum of flying cars above, they were here to shake hands.

“I don’t trust bureaucrats,” Rex said, offering his gloved hand.

“Neither do I,” rumbled the elephant through a vocoder embedded in its tusk.

Their hands — man and trunk — met in an electric clasp. Cameras floated around them, broadcasting the moment across solar systems. Two survivors of dead empires, forging a peace not through force, but through grit.

Somewhere in the distance, a synthwave melody pulsed from a noodle stall radio.

In the Blade Runner sprawl of humanity’s last cities, a new alliance was born — not from treaties or signatures, but from a handshake soaked in rain and lit by vaporwave dreams.
