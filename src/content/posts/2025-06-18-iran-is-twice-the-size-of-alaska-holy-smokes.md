Iran Is Twice the Size of Alaska?! Holy Smokes.
Posted by illphated | June 2025

I was today years old when I found out that Iran is TWICE the size of Alaska. Yes, the same Alaska that most of us already think is absolutely massive. Even crazier? It’s about two Texases stacked together—and if you're from Texas (or just proud of it), that’s a wild stat to wrap your head around.

In a neon-drenched late-night dive through obscure geography facts (as one does in a Blade Runner-style future), this one stopped me in my tracks. Iran clocks in at over 1.6 million square kilometers, while Alaska trails at just over 1.7 million square kilometers—but that includes a lot of frozen, uninhabitable land. When it comes to usable land mass and centrality in the global landscape, Iran is a giant.

Why Does This Matter?
Understanding a country's size isn't just for trivia night. Iran’s vast geography means:

Strategic positioning across Asia and the Middle East

Diverse climates ranging from snowy mountain ranges to deserts

Massive natural resource potential, including oil, gas, and minerals

A culture and history as expansive as its borders

It's a nation that’s long been at the crossroads of empires—and it's easy to forget just how physically big that influence spreads.
