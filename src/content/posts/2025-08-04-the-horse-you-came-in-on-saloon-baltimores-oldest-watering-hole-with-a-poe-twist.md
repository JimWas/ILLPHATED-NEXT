The Horse You Came In On Saloon – Baltimore’s Oldest Watering Hole With a Poe Twist
Baltimore is a city steeped in history — cobblestone streets, 18th-century architecture, and a harbor that helped shape America’s story. But few places bring that past to life quite like The Horse You Came In On Saloon.

Nestled in the heart of Fell’s Point, this unassuming brick-front tavern has been serving locals, sailors, and curious travelers since 1775. That’s right — it’s older than the United States itself.

From Colonial Tavern to Living Landmark
The Horse began life during colonial times as a waterfront tavern, serving rum and hearty meals to merchant sailors and shipbuilders. In those days, Fell’s Point was a bustling shipyard and a hub for maritime trade — and like any good port city, its taverns were as important as its docks.

What’s remarkable is that The Horse has never stopped pouring drinks. Even through Prohibition (1920–1933), when alcohol was banned nationwide, the saloon allegedly found ways to keep spirits flowing — quietly, of course. This makes it the oldest continuously operating saloon in the United States.

The Edgar Allan Poe Connection
The most famous chapter in The Horse’s history came in 1849, when literary legend Edgar Allan Poe spent his final hours in Baltimore. Local lore claims that The Horse was the last place Poe was seen alive before being found delirious on the city’s streets.

Today, the tavern embraces that connection — some even say Poe’s ghost still lingers, occasionally startling patrons with flickering lights, shifting chairs, and sudden chills.

Inside The Horse
Step through the doors and you’re met with creaking wood floors, exposed brick walls, and a bar that feels untouched by time. It’s easy to imagine 18th-century sailors, Civil War soldiers, and Poe himself sitting in the very same space.

The vibe is warm but buzzing — live music fills the air most nights, and the menu offers a mix of modern pub fare and Baltimore classics.

Why You Should Visit
Drink in history: You’re sipping in the same spot where people have gathered for nearly 250 years.

Poe lore: Perfect for fans of macabre history.

Baltimore’s nightlife: Fell’s Point comes alive after dark, and The Horse is at its heart.

If You Go
📍 Address: 1626 Thames St, Baltimore, MD 21231
⏰ Hours: Typically open late into the night — check ahead before visiting.
💡 Tip: Visit in the evening for live music, but swing by in the afternoon if you want to explore its history in a quieter setting.

The Horse You Came In On Saloon isn’t just a place to grab a drink — it’s a living museum of Baltimore’s maritime past, literary heritage, and enduring spirit. Whether you’re a history buff, a Poe enthusiast, or simply someone who appreciates a strong drink with a stronger story, you’ll find yourself right at home.
