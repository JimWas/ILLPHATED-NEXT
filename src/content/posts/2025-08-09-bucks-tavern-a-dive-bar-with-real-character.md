Buck’s Tavern: A Dive Bar with Real Character

There’s dive—and then there’s Buck’s. Locals hail Buck’s on Fulton as perhaps Evansville’s “diviest” dive bar. One Redditor quipped: “If your bikes are nice, I wouldn’t recommend taking them there. Someone might f*** with them.” Despite that tough exterior, Buck’s packs a community heart that keeps folks coming back.

History: Rooted in Fulton Avenue’s Legacy

Buck’s resides in a building steeped in history. Records show that the corner of Fulton and Indiana once held a saloon, a saddlery, a barber shop, and even the original Lamasco Bank around 1914. While Buck’s Tavern itself isn’t tied directly to those early businesses, it occupies a space that echoes Evansville’s evolving social fabric.

Tales from the Bar

Rough Around the Edges, Soft in the Middle
Locals describe Buck’s as gritty—but loyal. The dive bar aesthetic isn’t a facade; it’s real, and unapologetically so. That authenticity is part of its draw.

Friendly Vibe, Unexpected Stories
Despite the warnings for shiny bikes, regulars speak of genuine camaraderie inside. It’s a place where strangers slowly become familiar faces over cold beers and pool games.

Past Missteps, Present Respect
Buck’s did catch some flak in October 2018 when it was fined $750 for improperly refilling or misbranding alcohol bottles—a violation noted by the Indiana Liquor Control Commission. But to many regulars, these are just small bumps on a much longer road.

Why Buck’s Endures

For readers who dig authenticity, Buck’s is more than a bar—it’s a community artifact.

A Dive Bar Unpretentious and Real
It doesn’t try too hard. Buck’s is dive through and through, and that’s part of the draw.

History in Brick and Bottle
Situated on a corridor with tales dating back to the early 20th century, Buck’s is part of Evansville’s living history.

Stories You Won’t Find on the Menu
From scratched bikes to familiar faces, the memories made here form the pub’s true charm.
