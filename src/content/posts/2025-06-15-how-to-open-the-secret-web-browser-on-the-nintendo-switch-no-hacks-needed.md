How to Open the Secret Web Browser on the Nintendo Switch (No Hacks Needed)
If you’ve ever wished your Nintendo Switch had a built-in browser like your phone or tablet—good news: it does, but Nintendo just doesn’t want you to know.

🎮 Wait, the Switch Has a Browser?
Yes. While there’s no dedicated "Browser App" on the home screen, the Nintendo Switch does include a hidden web browser—used primarily for logging into public Wi-Fi. With a quick trick, you can access it yourself.

This method is 100% safe, doesn’t require homebrew, jailbreaking, or voiding your warranty. Just a clever workaround.

🧠 How to Access the Secret Web Browser on Nintendo Switch
✅ Step-by-Step Instructions
Power on your Switch and go to System Settings > Internet > Internet Settings.

Select your current Wi-Fi network, then choose Change Settings.

Scroll down to "DNS Settings" and change it from Automatic to Manual.

For Primary DNS, enter:

Copy
Edit
045.055.142.122
(This is a redirect server that triggers the browser.)

Leave Secondary DNS blank or set it to 0.0.0.0.

Save your settings and connect to the network again.

You’ll see a "Registration is required to use this network" message. Select Next.

💥 Boom! The secret Nintendo Switch browser will launch, allowing you to surf the web.

Access web-based games

⚠️ Keep in mind: not all websites will work perfectly. The browser is lightweight and lacks modern features like extensions or advanced media support.

🛑 How to Undo It (Optional)
If you're done browsing and want to go back to normal:

Go back to System Settings > Internet > Internet Settings

Select your Wi-Fi

Choose Change Settings

Set DNS Settings back to Automatic

Save and reconnect
