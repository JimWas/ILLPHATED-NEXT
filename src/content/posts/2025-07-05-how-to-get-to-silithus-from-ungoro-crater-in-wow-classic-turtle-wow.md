How to Get to Silithus from Un’Goro Crater in WoW Classic
If you’ve been exploring the lush jungle of Un’Goro Crater and are wondering how to make your way into the sun-scorched sands of Silithus, you’re not alone. Unlike other zones in World of Warcraft Classic, there’s no glowing portal or flight point connecting these two regions—it’s a subtle transition that many players miss.

Here’s your step-by-step guide to finding the entrance to Silithus from Un’Goro Crater.

🧭 Directions: Un’Goro to Silithus
Head to the western edge of Un’Goro Crater. You’re looking for a sloped, rocky area west of the central region, near the large Quillboar camp.

Walk up the rocky incline toward the mountainous ridge. As you climb, you’ll notice the environment begin to change—from steaming jungle to dry, cracked terrain.

Cross the ridge at around coordinates 47, 67 (Un’Goro side). Once you pass over the ridge, you’ll enter Silithus. Your minimap zone text should now display “Silithus.”

🏜 What to Expect
There’s no fanfare or portal—just a gradual change in scenery. You’ll move from the crater’s steaming heat vents and dinosaurs into the ominous silence of Silithus’s desert. It’s easy to miss if you’re not watching your minimap.

Pro Tip: If you’re heading to Cenarion Hold or prepping for AQ content, this is your natural land route. It’s also the only direct overland way into Silithus from the south.

⚠️ Common Confusion
“Why can’t I find the entrance?”
You’re likely not walking far enough up the slope. It’s a natural transition—no dungeon-like portal or guard post.

“Is there a flight path nearby?”
Not in Un’Goro. You’ll find the Silithus flight point in Cenarion Hold, near the center of the zone once you’re inside.

“What level should I be?”
Silithus is generally meant for level 55-60 players. Be ready for elites and bugs.

🗺 Final Notes
The journey from Un’Goro to Silithus is one of the more immersive zone transitions in WoW Classic. You feel the change in climate and danger. It’s perfect for players chasing quests, exploring lore, or just wanting to see what lies beyond the crater.

So grab your gear, mount up, and take the plunge westward—Silithus awaits.
