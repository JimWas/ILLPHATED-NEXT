Where to Exchange US Dollars to Japanese Yen in Edogawa, Tokyo

If you just landed in Tokyo and find yourself wandering through the calm neighborhoods of Edogawa, you’ll notice something right away — this isn’t Shibuya or Shinjuku. It’s quieter here. More local. The neon hum fades into small cafés, family-run ramen shops, and bike lanes leading to the river.

But when your wallet’s full of greenbacks and you need yen to ride the train, grab sushi, or hit a konbini, you’ve gotta know where to exchange your money without getting burned.

Here’s the real guide — no tourist traps, no mystery fees — just the best spots to exchange USD for JPY in Edogawa.

🏦 1. Daikokuya Nishikoiwa

📍 Address: 2F, 1-Chome-23-1 Nishikoiwa, Edogawa City, Tokyo 133-0057
📞 Phone: +81 3-5612-0521
💡 Hours: Typically 10:00 – 18:00 (closed on some holidays)

Daikokuya is Tokyo’s go-to currency exchange chain — fast, legit, and straightforward. The Nishikoiwa branch sits right in Edogawa, making it perfect if you’re staying near Koiwa or Ichinoe.

They handle USD → JPY on the spot, usually with better rates than banks. Just bring your passport or ID (required for larger exchanges).

Pro tip: Always ask about the spread (difference between buy/sell rates) — a few yen can add up fast.

🤖 2. Smart Exchange Machine – Nishikasai

📍 Address: 6-Chome-19-18 Nishikasai, Edogawa City, Tokyo 134-0088
🕓 Open: 24 hours (self-service)

These futuristic little kiosks are popping up all over Japan — and Edogawa’s got one tucked near Nishikasai Station.

They work like an ATM: insert your USD bills, and the machine spits out crisp yen. The interface supports English, Chinese, and Korean.

Pros:
✅ No human interaction needed.
✅ Operates 24/7.
✅ Transparent rates shown before confirming.

Cons:
⚠️ Only accepts certain bill types (no old or damaged notes).
⚠️ Slightly higher spread than in-person counters.

Perfect for night owls or solo travelers who want quick cash with zero hassle.

💼 3. World Currency Shop (MUFG Group)

🌐 Website: tokyo-card.co.jp/wcs/en

This chain is run by Mitsubishi UFJ Financial Group, one of Japan’s largest banks. They’ve got branches across Tokyo — including some near Edogawa — and offer great reliability.

They post daily exchange rates online, so you can compare before heading out. Their staff speaks basic English, and they accept major currencies like USD, EUR, GBP, and AUD.

If you’re heading toward Kasai, Kinshicho, or even Tokyo Station, you’ll likely spot one nearby.

💬 Quick Tips Before You Exchange

Compare rates first. Check Wise.com
 or the Bank of Japan’s daily rate
 to avoid bad deals.

Ask about fees. Some spots charge a flat commission or set minimum exchange amounts.

Bring your passport. Required by law for certain transaction sizes.

Exchange in daylight. Most shops close by 6 PM — machines are your late-night backup.
