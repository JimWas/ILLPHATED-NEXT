Jacksonville’s Darkest Secret: The Taxi Driver Who Hunted Women at Night

By Illphated | August 7, 2025 | True Crime / Horror / Florida Weirdness

🚨 TRIGGER WARNING: This article contains disturbing true crime content. Reader discretion is advised. 🚨

He looked like just another cab driver…
But Paul Durousseau wasn’t just taking you home.
He was taking you to HELL.

Welcome to Illphated.com, where we expose the underbelly of America one horrifying secret at a time. Today’s nightmare takes us deep into the humid heart of Jacksonville, Florida—where a smooth-talking ex-soldier turned cab driver quietly became one of the most twisted serial killers the city has ever seen.

Meet Paul Durousseau — a man who weaponized trust, uniforms, and even his job behind the wheel of a Gator City taxi to commit some of the most gut-wrenching crimes in recent memory.

🧠 The Mask of Normalcy: Who Was Paul Durousseau?
To the outside world, Paul was just another guy trying to make ends meet.
An Army vet. A husband. A father.
Hell, even a cab driver. Friendly. Helpful.

But behind that quiet smile?

A monster was stalking the streets.

Durousseau used his taxi license like a hunting permit, preying on women who were young, vulnerable—and often pregnant.

Yes, pregnant.
His victims were literally carrying life…
…when he choked the life out of them.

🗡️ The Killings: Slow, Silent, and Personal
Between 1997 and 2003, Durousseau committed at least 6 murders—but the true number may never be known.

Here’s the chilling pattern:

Lure the victim into his car (some were even neighbors or acquaintances).

Rape them—often in their own homes.

Strangle them with objects at hand: belts, phone cords, even their own clothing.

One of the worst parts?

He’d often stay in the home afterward…
…watching TV, relaxing, like nothing happened.

📍 Jacksonville: A City Held Hostage by Fear
While the beaches glimmered and Jaguars fans chanted, a serial killer was quietly tearing apart families across the city.
Women began to vanish.
The media failed to connect the dots.
Cops weren’t seeing the pattern.

Why?

Because Durousseau was hiding in plain sight.
He was the neighbor. The driver.
The one holding open the door with a smile.

⚖️ The Downfall: Finally Caught, But Not Forgotten
Durousseau was finally arrested in 2003.
His DNA linked him to multiple unsolved homicides.
He was charged, tried, and sentenced to death.

But the damage was done.
Jacksonville would never be the same.

To this day, he's sitting on death row—still breathing while his victims are buried.
