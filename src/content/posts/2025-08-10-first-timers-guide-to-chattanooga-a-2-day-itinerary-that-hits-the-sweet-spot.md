First-Timer’s Guide to Chattanooga: A 2-Day Itinerary That Hits the Sweet Spot
Chattanooga, Tennessee. The name sounds like a train whistle, and honestly, that’s the right vibe — this city hums with history, natural beauty, and a gritty southern charm that’ll hook you faster than a Tennessee River catfish.

Whether you’re rolling in from Atlanta, Nashville, or a little farther afield, this first-timer’s guide will give you a killer two-day plan that blends the classics with a few Illphated-approved curveballs.

Day 1 – Downtown & Riverfront Magic
Morning – Coffee, Aquarium, and a Walk Along the River

Start with Mean Mug Coffeehouse or Frothy Monkey — artisanal caffeine is a must before you start exploring.

Head to the Tennessee Aquarium, one of the best in the country, with two massive buildings dedicated to River and Ocean ecosystems. Even if you’re “not an aquarium person,” you will be after this.

Stroll the Tennessee Riverwalk afterward — bridges, public art, and killer views.

Lunch – Southern Comfort

Hit Maple Street Biscuit Company for next-level biscuit sandwiches.

If you want BBQ, Champy’s Famous Fried Chicken or Sugar’s Ribs will have you questioning every diet you ever tried.

Afternoon – Museums & Murals

Check out the Hunter Museum of American Art for a dose of culture with your southern hospitality.

Wander around Main Street to spot Chattanooga’s vibrant street art — the murals here turn alleys into open-air galleries.

Evening – Sunset & Scenic Views

Take the Incline Railway up to Lookout Mountain for panoramic views that feel like the city’s throwing its arms open just for you.

If you want to keep it urban, watch the sunset from Walnut Street Bridge, one of the longest pedestrian bridges in the world.

Dinner – Elevated but Chill

STIR for oysters and craft cocktails, or Public House for farm-to-table southern dishes done right.

End the night with a drink at Whiskey Thief, Chattanooga’s rooftop bar that lets you sip bourbon with a skyline view.

Day 2 – Nature & Neighborhood Vibes
Morning – Pancakes or Pastries

Grab breakfast at Bluegrass Grill (their pancakes are the size of your head) or Rembrandt’s Coffee House for something lighter.

Midday – Outdoor Adventures

Rock City Gardens – Yes, it’s touristy, but the trails, rock formations, and “See Seven States” view are worth it.

Or hit Ruby Falls, an underground waterfall that looks like nature’s version of a neon concert.

Lunch – Local Favorites

Try Aretha Frankenstein’s for quirky atmosphere and stacked breakfast-all-day plates.

Or Taco Mamacita for a fun, fast Tex-Mex fix.

Afternoon – Neighborhood Hopping

Explore North Shore, a hip district full of boutiques, galleries, and Coolidge Park, where you can ride an antique carousel.

If you’re into craft beer, OddStory Brewing Co. or Hutton & Smith will treat you right.

Evening – Wrap It Up in Style

Dinner at Alleia, a rustic Italian spot with a romantic vibe, or Feed Co. Table & Tavern for a lively atmosphere and great comfort food.

End your trip with live music — Songbirds is a great spot for local and touring acts, while JJ’s Bohemia gives you gritty dive bar energy with killer sets.

Quick Illphated Tips
Stay Downtown so you can walk to most attractions — the Read House hotel is haunted in the best way.

Chattanooga’s free electric shuttle makes getting around easy.

The city is surprisingly bike-friendly — rent a bike for the Riverwalk.

Bring a light jacket; even in summer, nights by the river can be breezy.

Bottom line:
Two days in Chattanooga will have you chasing waterfalls, inhaling biscuits, and staring out over mountaintops wondering if you should just stay. And honestly… you probably should.
