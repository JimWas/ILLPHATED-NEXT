Countries Whose Flags Are Red, White, and Blue
From the stars and stripes of the United States to the tricolor banners of France and the Netherlands, the colors red, white, and blue have been used in national flags around the world for centuries. These three colors are often associated with ideals such as freedom, unity, and resilience — but each nation has its own unique history and symbolism behind the palette.

Why These Colors?
Red, white, and blue have become popular for their strong visual contrast and powerful symbolism:

Red often represents bravery, strength, or the blood of those who fought for the country.

White symbolizes purity, peace, or unity.

Blue can stand for freedom, loyalty, or the sky and sea.

A Global Connection
While each flag’s design is distinct, this shared color combination creates an unexpected visual link between countries that are worlds apart — from European kingdoms to Pacific island nations.
