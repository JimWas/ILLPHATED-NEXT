# Free and Paid SMS Verification Services

## Free Options

### [smspinverify.com](https://smspinverify.com/free-number)
Offers both free and paid services. The free service works well for platforms like WhatsApp, Burner, and some financial sites like PayPal. The numbers are exclusively from the USA and are generally not burner numbers.

### [Tempsmss.com](https://tempsmss.com)
Great for creating Telegram accounts, but you must be the first to use the number. Follow their [Telegram account](https://t.me/tempsmss) to catch new numbers early.

### [sms-activate.guru](https://sms-activate.guru/en/freeNumbers)
Works for platforms like Tinder, Gmail, Instagram, and TikTok. However, it only provides numbers from Asian and European countries, with no availability for USA/Canada.

### [anonymsms.com](https://anonymsms.com)
Ideal for less common services.

### [Textrapp.com](https://textrapp.com)
Offers numbers from USA, Canada, Netherlands, UK, and Sweden. Best for a few well-known platforms.

### [MobileSMS.io](https://mobilesms.io)
Similar to Textrapp in functionality.

### [Quackr.io](https://quackr.io)
Provides numbers from multiple countries with a great frontend. However, numbers get burned quickly, and a free account is required.

### [SMSPOOL.net](https://smspool.net)
Currently offers free numbers but is limited to French, Italian, Latvian, Swedish, and Estonian numbers.

### [receive-sms-online.info](https://receive-sms-online.info)
Free numbers for less popular platforms.

### [smstome.com](https://smstome.com)
Useful for verifying less common platforms but unreliable for financial or dating services. Messages are public.

## Paid Options

### [smspinverify.com](https://smspinverify.com)
Offers competitive pricing and multiple payment methods, including crypto. Features include rental numbers and priority numbers for services like Google Voice, Cash App, eBay, and PayPal.

### [majorphones.com](https://majorphones.com)
Well-known for high-quality numbers. Provides short-term, middle-term (1-day use), and long-term (30-day) numbers. Prices range from $0.20 to $12. Reviews available on [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/majorphones.com) and [Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/PrivacyGuides/comments/137arb9/effective_sms_verification_guide/).

### [smsotpverify.com](https://smsotpverify.com)
Supports platforms like Amazon, WhatsApp, Facebook, and Telegram. API available. Payments via credit card and crypto. Prices start at $0.20, with WhatsApp numbers costing $4.88.

### [smspool.com](https://smspool.com)
Offers affordable numbers across many countries. Payment via cryptocurrency and Visa/Mastercard (via Stripe). Has a strong API but high demand may result in reused numbers.

### [textverified.com](https://textverified.com)
Mixed reviews on [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/www.textverified.com). Some users find it reliable, while others report issues with number rejection.

### [non-voip.com](https://non-voip.com)
High-quality numbers for Telegram, Google, and Google Voice. API is unreliable. Payment is crypto-only.

### [smspva.com](https://smspva.com)
Cheap but often burned numbers. Offers many countries, but long-term numbers are unreliable.

### [tardigrada.io](https://tardigrada.io)
Similar to smsotpverify.com.

### [mmohub.io](https://mmohub.io)
Good for Tinder, but the website is in Vietnamese (use Google Translate).

### [sms-verify.me](https://sms-verify.me)
Good for PayPal.

### [sms-activate.guru](https://sms-activate.guru)
Paid plan is reliable and automated. Refunds balance when numbers don't work.

### [verifysms.io](https://verifysms.io)
Alternative to textverified. Only provides USA numbers for 80 services.

### [anosim.net](https://anosim.net)
Offers physical SIM cards for rent.

### [codesverify.com](https://codesverify.com)
Alternative to smspva.com.

### [smscode.net](https://smscode.net)
Private pool of SIM cards, works well for short-term numbers.
