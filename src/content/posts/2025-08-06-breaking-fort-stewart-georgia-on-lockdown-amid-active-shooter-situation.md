🚨 Breaking: Fort Stewart, Georgia on Lockdown Amid Active Shooter Situation
Fort Stewart, Georgia — one of the largest U.S. Army installations east of the Mississippi — is currently on lockdown following reports of an active shooter, according to military police.

The official Fort Stewart-Hunter Army Airfield Facebook page confirmed that casualties have been reported and the situation remains ongoing. Law enforcement and emergency crews are on the scene, and residents have been urged to shelter in place. The lockdown began at 11:04 a.m.

“There is an active shooter,” a Fort Stewart Police Department spokesperson confirmed. “The situation is still developing.”

📍 About Fort Stewart
Fort Stewart isn’t just any Army base — it’s a sprawling military community located about 40 miles southwest of Savannah. The installation covers roughly 280,000 acres, making it the largest military installation by area in the eastern United States.

It serves as the home of the 3rd Infantry Division, known for its distinguished combat history dating back to World War I. The post trains and deploys both active duty and reserve units and plays a critical role in rapid military readiness.

Fort Stewart is also unique in that it houses a blended population of over 10,000 soldiers, families, and civilian employees who live and work on post. It’s a small city in itself — complete with schools, housing areas, training ranges, and airfields.
