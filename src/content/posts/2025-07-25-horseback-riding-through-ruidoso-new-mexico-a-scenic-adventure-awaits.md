Horseback Riding Through Ruidoso, New Mexico: A Scenic Adventure Awaits


Nestled in the Sierra Blanca mountains of southern New Mexico, Ruidoso offers a unique blend of natural beauty and rich history, making it an ideal destination for horseback riding enthusiasts. Whether you're a seasoned rider or a beginner, Ruidoso's diverse trails and scenic vistas promise an unforgettable equestrian experience.

🐎 Top Horseback Riding Destinations in Ruidoso
1. Grindstone Stables
Located at 523 Resort Dr., Grindstone Stables offers guided one-hour trail rides suitable for all skill levels. The trail meanders past Grindstone Lake and ascends to the top of Wrangler Ridge, covering approximately 3 miles. Riders can enjoy opportunities for wildlife viewing and photography along the way. The stable is known for its friendly and knowledgeable guides, ensuring a memorable experience. Reservations are recommended, and the cost is $60 per rider, with a $10 discount for cash payments .

2. Inn of the Mountain Gods
Situated in nearby Mescalero, the Inn of the Mountain Gods offers horseback riding experiences with breathtaking views. Trail rides depart daily at 9 am, 11 am, and 1 pm. Riders can choose between 1-hour, 2-hour, half-day, or full-day rides, with prices ranging from $60 to $450 per person . The resort also provides boarding services for travelers bringing their own horses.

4. Lincoln National Forest
The Lincoln National Forest encompasses over 1.1 million acres of diverse terrain, offering numerous trails for horseback riding. Riders can explore areas like the White Mountain Wilderness, known for its scenic beauty and abundant wildlife . Please note that some areas may have restrictions, so it's advisable to check with the U.S. Forest Service for current trail conditions and regulations.

🌄 What to Expect on the Trails
Ruidoso's trails offer a variety of landscapes, from dense forests to open meadows, providing riders with a chance to connect with nature. The cooler temperatures, especially in the higher elevations, make for comfortable riding conditions. Wildlife sightings are common, with opportunities to see species such as mule deer, elk, and various bird species. The trails are generally well-maintained, but it's essential to be prepared for changing weather conditions, especially during the monsoon season.

🧳 Tips for a Safe and Enjoyable Ride
Wear appropriate attire: Long pants and closed-toe shoes are recommended.

Bring essentials: Carry water, sunscreen, and a hat for sun protection.

Check weather conditions: Be aware of the forecast, as afternoon thunderstorms can occur during the summer months.

Follow guide instructions: Always adhere to the guidance of your trail guide to ensure safety.

📅 Plan Your Adventure
Ruidoso's horseback riding season typically spans from spring through fall, with the most favorable conditions in late spring and early fall when temperatures are moderate. It's advisable to make reservations in advance, especially during peak tourist seasons, to secure your spot.
