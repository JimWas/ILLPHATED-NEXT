When boxed cake mixes were first developed in the 1930s and 1940s, they originally contained powdered eggs, meaning users only needed to add water. However, in the 1950s, marketing research suggested that consumers felt more involved and connected to the baking process when they had to add fresh ingredients themselves—especially eggs.
