Inflation Hurts Everyone But the Rich – Here's Why

Walk into a Walmart today and try not to choke when you see an Arizona Iced Tea for $10.99. What used to be a symbol of stability — that “Still 99¢” can — now looks like a cruel joke. Welcome to the economy of 2025.

Inflation is everywhere. It’s in your groceries, your rent, your gas, your medicine, and maybe even your iced tea. But here’s the twisted part: while regular people are scraping coins together, the rich are not just surviving — they’re thriving.

The Rich Don’t Feel It Like You Do

When prices go up, you cut back. You skip meals, delay the dentist, stretch your paycheck past the breaking point. The rich? They just move money. They shift assets, buy up undervalued property, invest in rising markets, and pass along higher costs to someone else — usually you.

They aren’t affected by $6 eggs or $300 electric bills. They own the grocery stores. They own the power companies. Inflation doesn’t touch them — it feeds them.

Your Raise Is a Mirage

Maybe your boss gave you a 3% raise. Cool. But inflation rose 7%. Congrats — you’re now making less than last year. While billionaires get richer from price hikes and stock buybacks, your paycheck is worth less each day. And if you complain? They tell you to budget better or stop buying lattes.

Why Is This Happening?

It’s not just “global supply chains” or “geopolitical instability.” It’s a system rigged to protect capital, not people. When the cost of living spikes, you suffer. When the cost of doing business spikes, the rich get bailouts, subsidies, and tax breaks.

They’ll tell you it’s complicated. It’s not. The wealthy own the game. You’re just playing in it — and the rules change every time you get close to winning.

What Can We Do?

Start by recognizing the scam. Educate yourself. Support policies that rein in corporate greed. Vote for people who serve the public, not lobbyists. And never stop calling it what it is: economic warfare disguised as market fluctuation.

Because if we don’t, we’ll all keep sipping overpriced iced tea while billionaires toast to your silence.
