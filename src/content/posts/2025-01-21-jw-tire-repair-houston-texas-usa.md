Putting a tire on a rim can be a bit tricky, but with the right tools and technique, it’s definitely doable! Here’s a step-by-step guide:

 

### Tools You’ll Need:

1. **Tire iron or pry bar**: To help remove the old tire.

2. **Lubricant**: To make the process smoother (dish soap and water work well).

3. **Tire bead breaker**: To break the seal between the tire and rim (optional but helpful).

4. **Air compressor**: To inflate the tire after installation.

 

### Steps:

1. **Prepare the Rim and Tire**:

- Remove the old tire if it’s still on the rim. Use the tire iron to pry off the tire bead.

- Clean the rim thoroughly to ensure a proper seal.

 

2. **Lubricate the Tire Bead**:

- Apply lubricant to the tire bead and rim. This helps the tire slide onto the rim more easily.

 

3. **Position the Tire on the Rim**:

- Place the tire on the rim at an angle. The bottom bead should start to slide over the lip of the rim.

 

4. **Install the Tire**:

- Using a tire iron, carefully work the tire around the rim. Be patient and work slowly to avoid damaging the tire or rim.

- Once the bottom bead is on, repeat the process for the top bead.

 

5. **Inflate the Tire**:

- Use the air compressor to inflate the tire. Ensure the bead seats properly against the rim.

- Inflate to the recommended PSI listed on the tire.
