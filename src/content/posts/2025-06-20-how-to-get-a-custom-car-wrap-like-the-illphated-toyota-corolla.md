🧠 Step 1: Finalize the Design

You’ve already got your design (like the ILLPHATED.com wrapped Corolla in that neon-drenched future city). Make sure you:
	•	Use a high-resolution version of the artwork.
	•	Save it in vector format if possible (AI, EPS, or PDF).
	•	Ensure it fits the exact model of your vehicle. Wraps must account for body curves, door handles, windows, etc.

🛠 Tip: Ask a wrap designer to generate a vehicle template mockup for your exact year and model of Toyota Corolla (e.g., “2022 Corolla Hatchback Wrap Template”).

💵 Step 3: Understand the Cost

For a full car wrap with a custom print:
	•	Price Range: $2,000 – $4,000
	•	Factors include size of your car, material used, labor, and whether you need them to print the design or just install.

Tip: If you already have the artwork ready (like your ILLPHATED design), you might save up to $500–$1,000 on design fees.

⸻

📦 Step 4: Print and Install the Wrap

Once the design is approved:
	1.	The shop prints the wrap on large-format vinyl printers.
	2.	They prep your car (wash, decontaminate, maybe remove trim).
	3.	The wrap is applied with heat guns and squeegees, panel by panel.
	4.	Curing time: You may need to wait 24–48 hours before washing.

⸻

🧼 Step 5: Maintain That Blade Runner Look
	•	Hand wash only with soft cloth and wrap-safe soap.
	•	Avoid high-pressure washers.
	•	Park in shade or garage to extend wrap life (typically 5–7 years).
