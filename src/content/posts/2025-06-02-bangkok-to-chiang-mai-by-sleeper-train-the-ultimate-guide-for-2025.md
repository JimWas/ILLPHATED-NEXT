Bangkok to Chiang Mai by Sleeper Train: The Ultimate Guide for 2025

Looking for a unique, affordable, and comfortable way to travel from Bangkok to Chiang Mai? Take the sleeper train! This scenic overnight journey is one of the best travel experiences in Thailand. Whether you're a backpacker, digital nomad, or adventure-seeking traveler, here's everything you need to know before boarding the overnight sleeper train from Bangkok to Chiang Mai.

Why Take the Sleeper Train from Bangkok to Chiang Mai?

The overnight train is a smart choice for several reasons:

Save on hotel costs — Sleep while you travel.

Enjoy beautiful countryside views — Wake up to rice fields and misty mountains.

Eco-friendly — Trains are a greener alternative to short domestic flights.

Comfortable and safe — Modern sleeper trains have improved dramatically.



---

🛤️ Train Schedule & Route

Trains depart daily from Krung Thep Aphiwat Central Terminal in Bangkok to Chiang Mai Railway Station. Two of the most popular options:

Train No. 9 – Departs 18:40, arrives 07:15 (modern train with first-class and second-class A/C sleepers)

Train No. 13 – Departs 20:05, arrives 08:40 (classic but comfy sleeper train)


Both trains travel along Thailand’s Northern Line, covering roughly 700 km.


---

🛏️ Sleeper Train Options: First Class vs. Second Class

🚪 First Class Sleeper (Private Cabins):

Lockable 2-berth rooms

Air-conditioned

Power outlets & reading lights

Room service-style food ordering

Price: ฿1,453–฿2,453 THB


🛌 Second Class Sleeper (Open Compartments):

Curtain-covered bunks for privacy

A/C and fan options

Lower berths more spacious than upper

Shared restrooms

Price: ฿941–฿1,041 THB



---

🎟️ How to Book Your Sleeper Train Tickets

✅ Official Booking

Use the State Railway of Thailand’s D-Ticket system (book up to 180 days in advance).


✅ Trusted Third-Party Sites

12Go Asia: Easy to use, English interface, small fee.


✅ In-Person

Buy directly at major train stations in Bangkok.


💡 Pro Tip: First-class cabins sell out fast, especially in high season (Nov–Feb). Book early!


---

🌄 What to Expect Onboard

Beds come with clean linen, pillow, and blanket.

Food and drink are available—either in the dining car or delivered to your berth.

Expect a bumpy ride—pack earplugs if you're a light sleeper.

Bathrooms are clean but basic (hot water in First Class only).

Bring a sweater—it gets chilly with A/C.



---

📸 Is the Sleeper Train Worth It?

Absolutely. It’s not just transportation—it’s a travel experience. You'll meet fellow travelers, see a side of Thailand most tourists miss, and arrive rested and ready to explore Chiang Mai.


---

🧭 Final Tips for Your Journey

Bring snacks and water (station vendors are cheaper).

Arrive 30 minutes early at the station.

Don’t forget your passport (they check it at boarding).

Charge your devices in advance (outlets are limited in Second Class).
