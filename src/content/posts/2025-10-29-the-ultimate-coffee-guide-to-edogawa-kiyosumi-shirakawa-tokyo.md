The Ultimate Coffee Guide to Edogawa & Kiyosumi-Shirakawa, Tokyo

Tokyo is a city that loves its coffee culture — from sleek international chains to hidden independent roasters. If you’re exploring the quieter streets of Edogawa or the specialty coffee hub of Kiyosumi-Shirakawa, we’ve got you covered with the best cafés for your caffeine fix.

⸻

Edogawa’s Neighborhood Coffee Gems

While Edogawa isn’t as famous as Shibuya or Shinjuku for coffee, it’s full of cozy local spots perfect for a morning pick-me-up or a quiet afternoon break.

1. Edogawa Coffee Company

Nestled in the Ichinoe neighborhood, Edogawa Coffee Company is a small but inviting café that focuses on quality beans and a relaxed atmosphere. Perfect for working remotely or enjoying a slow coffee moment.

📍 7‑33‑19 Ichinoe, Edogawa City, Tokyo 132‑0024
🌐 Tabelog listing

⸻

2. Point Two / Gobuichi Coffee

Stylish and minimal, Point Two / Gobuichi Coffee is ideal for those looking for an aesthetic café vibe. Their carefully brewed coffee pairs perfectly with Instagram-worthy interiors.

📍 2‑17‑12 Matsushima, Edogawa City, Tokyo 132‑0031

⸻

3. HealthyTOKYO CBD Cafe & Factory Edogawa

For something a little different, check out HealthyTOKYO CBD Cafe & Factory. This niche café offers CBD-infused drinks and vegan treats, making it a unique coffee experience in Tokyo.

📍 1‑18‑8 Chūō, Edogawa City, Tokyo 132‑0021
🌐 Visit HealthyTOKYO

⸻

Kiyosumi-Shirakawa: Tokyo’s Specialty Coffee Hub

Kiyosumi-Shirakawa is where Tokyo’s coffee enthusiasts go to explore specialty roasters and unique café experiences.

4. Blue Bottle Coffee – Kiyosumi-Shirakawa Flagship

Blue Bottle is a global favorite, and the Kiyosumi-Shirakawa location does not disappoint. Expect meticulously brewed coffee, minimalist décor, and a space perfect for coffee lovers.

📍 1‑4‑8 Hirano, Koto City, Tokyo 135‑0023
🌐 Blue Bottle Coffee Japan

⸻

5. Allpress Espresso Tokyo Roastery & Cafe

Allpress Espresso offers a warehouse-style café with expertly roasted beans. It’s ideal if you’re serious about your coffee and want a strong, flavorful cup.

📍 3‑7‑2 Hirano, Koto City, Tokyo 135‑0023
🌐 Allpress Espresso

⸻

6. Arise Coffee Roasters

Independent and inviting, Arise Coffee Roasters is a local favorite for those who enjoy quiet cafés and expertly brewed coffee. Its unassuming location hides a true gem for coffee aficionados.

📍 1‑13‑8 Hirano, Koto City, Tokyo 135‑0023
🌐 Arise Coffee

⸻

☕ Tips for Your Coffee Crawl
	•	Timing is everything: Most cafés open around 9–10 AM and can get busy on weekends.
	•	Explore small streets: Many Edogawa and Kiyosumi cafés are tucked away. Use Google Maps or Tabelog to navigate.
	•	Specialty experiences: Kiyosumi-Shirakawa is perfect for pour-over enthusiasts, while Edogawa is better for quiet, cozy coffee moments.
